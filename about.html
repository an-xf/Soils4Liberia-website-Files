About The Project

The aim of Soils4Liberia project is to improve the management and use of land and soil resources for resource conservation, sustainable intensification of agricultural production and enhanced resilience to climate change. This will be achieved by improved policy formulation and improved decision making on stakeholder interventions based on reliable information on Liberia’s land and soil resources as well as recommendations provided by the project. Therefore, the impact indicators will be policies and action plans for resource conservation, agricultural development for prioritised value chains (e.g., rice) and mitigation of and adaptation to climate change (climate-smart agriculture) that will make use of the land and soil data provided by this project.
​
The project’s specific objectives will include:
  • Establish a fully functional Land and Soil Information System that contains reliable primary current data and that facilitates managing soil data and information and generating information products according to user requirements.
  • Raise stakeholders’ awareness of and insight in the value of land and soil data and information and capacitate them in using such information for their decisions regarding improving land and soil resources use and management and enhanced soil-related ecosystems services for intensified agricultural production, improved climate resilience and better protection of the land and soil resources.
  • Establish a functional centre that generates and manages relevant and reliable information on Liberia’s land and soil resources to inform policy formulation, planning of interventions and execution of actions for agricultural development, mitigation of and adaptation to climate change and resources conservation, including strengthening capacity in Liberia to carry out soil surveys and land use inventories autonomously.
From the onset, the project will engage with stakeholders in view of specific objective two. Stakeholder consultation serves two purposes:
  • to get insight in the requirements for soil-related data and information, as well as in the capacity of the stakeholder community to use such data and information effectively, and
  • to define so called “use cases” through which stakeholders will be involved in developing the practical application of soil data and information for their own purpose. As to the first purpose, insights in the user requirements for soil information will determine the data and information products to be generated and the specifications for those products. This, in turn, will inform the design of the LSIS in the broader sense, including the design of the field campaign and specification of the data that need to be collected (including the desired soil analyses), as well as the design of the workflows and data processes to generate the required information.
